# Sources — 03 Voice and Perspective

## Primary citations

- Natsume Sōseki, _Wagahai wa Neko de Aru_ (_I Am a Cat_), serialized in _Hototogisu_ 1905–06; English trans. Aiko Itō and Graeme Wilson (Tuttle, 2002). Source for the canonical persona-writing example: high-register first-person pronoun (`wagahai`) used by an unnamed stray cat narrator. Wikipedia overview: <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/I_Am_a_Cat>. LitHub essay: <https://lithub.com/on-sosekis-bitingly-critical-novel-i-am-a-cat/>.
- Google developer documentation style guide, "Voice and tone" and "Person." <https://developers.google.com/style/tone>. Source for second-person, present-tense, active-voice as the default for technical instruction.
- Microsoft Writing Style Guide. Source for "write like you speak" and second-person default. Public version: <https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/style-guide/welcome/>.
- MasterClass, "What Is Second Person Point of View in Writing?" — practical guide. <https://www.masterclass.com/articles/what-is-second-person-point-of-view-in-writing-how-to-writer-in-second-person-narrative-voice-with-examples>.

## Notes for editors

The six dials of persona writing (identity, register, vocabulary fingerprint, sentence shape, stance, relationship to the reader) are synthesized from Sōseki's worked example and the brand-archetype framework in `front-end/references/02-brand-and-copywriting.md`. When extending this chapter, prefer worked examples from named published work (Sōseki, Didion, Sedaris, Lewis, Tolentino) over invented examples.

The "Don't strip the human" section connects directly to AI DNA detection in chapter 08 and in front-end/02 § AI copy DNA. Cross-reference rather than duplicate.
