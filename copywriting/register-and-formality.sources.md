# Sources — 02 Register and Formality

## Primary citations

- Martin Joos, _The Five Clocks: A Linguistic Excursion into the Five Styles of English Usage_ (Harcourt, Brace & World, 1962). Source for the five-register taxonomy (frozen, formal, consultative, casual, intimate). Wikipedia overview: <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Martin_Joos>. Register sociolinguistics overview: <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Register_(sociolinguistics)>.
- Cambridge Grammar of English, "Formal and informal language." <https://dictionary.cambridge.org/grammar/british-grammar/formal-and-informal-language>. Source for formal/informal contrasts in vocabulary, contractions, and pronouns.
- Ward Farnsworth, _Farnsworth's Classical English Style_ (Godine, 2020). Source for the Anglo-Saxon vs Latinate distinction in modern English usage. Summary: <https://davidlabaree.com/2020/08/20/farnsworth-on-balancing-saxon-and-latinate-words-in-your-writing/>.

## Secondary references

- Stringham, "Understanding Registers of Language for Consecutive Interpreting," intrpr.info: <http://intrpr.info/library/stringham-understanding-register-formality.pdf>. Practical breakdown of Joos's registers in interpretation contexts.
- Oxford Reference, "Formality": <https://www.oxfordreference.com/display/10.1093/oi/authority.20111111212622150>.
- David Welch, "English is made of Saxon words and Latin words. Here's how to use them.": <https://medium.com/@watsonwelch/english-is-made-of-saxon-words-and-latin-words-heres-how-to-use-them-56e3a079b96>.
- Derek Haines, "Using Register In English Writing And How To Control It": <https://medium.com/@Derek_Haines/using-register-in-english-writing-and-how-to-control-it-f5466e5fda9>.

## Notes for editors

The five-register taxonomy is treated as canonical in linguistics. Register-marker tables are synthesized from Cambridge Grammar contrasts and Farnsworth — when adding new markers, cite both an academic linguistics source and at least one professional style guide that operationalizes the marker.
