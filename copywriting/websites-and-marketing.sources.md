# Sources — 05 Websites and Marketing

## Primary citations

- David Ogilvy, _Ogilvy on Advertising_ (Crown, 1983). Source for the principle that specificity beats superlatives in advertising and that ad copy should earn the next click. Canonical reference for direct response and brand advertising craft.
- Eugene Schwartz, _Breakthrough Advertising_ (Bottom Line Books, 1966; reprinted 2017). Source for awareness-stage matching of message specificity to audience sophistication.
- Joanna Wiebe, _Copyhackers_ (copyhackers.com). Source for voice-of-customer-driven landing pages and conversion-focused testing methodology.
- Google Ads Help, "Write effective search ads." <https://support.google.com/google-ads/answer/1704392>. Source for the 2026 character constraints (30/90/300) and best practices for responsive search ads.
- Meta Ads, "Best practices for ad copy." <https://www.facebook.com/business/help/980593475366490>. Source for Meta-specific text length recommendations and creative principles.

## Secondary references

- LinkedIn Marketing Solutions, "Sponsored Content character limits": <https://business.linkedin.com/marketing-solutions/native-advertising>.
- Litmus, "The science of subject lines": <https://www.litmus.com/blog/the-science-of-email-subject-lines/>.
- Mailchimp Content Style Guide. <https://styleguide.mailchimp.com/>. Cross-reference for marketing-email voice; voice-vs-tone framework cited in `front-end/brand-and-copywriting.md`.
- AAAA / IAB OOH guidelines on copy length and visibility windows. <https://www.aaaa.org/>.

## Curated tool catalog

- Litmus / Email on Acid — preview email rendering across clients before sending; spam-filter scoring.
- Hemingway Editor — short-form copy reading-grade check, particularly useful for ad headlines.
- Frase / Clearscope — search-intent analysis for landing-page copy aligned with search ads.
- Headline Studio (CoSchedule) — programmatic headline scoring; useful as a check, not a target.

## Notes for editors

This chapter intentionally points to `front-end/brand-and-copywriting.md` for web-specific patterns. When extending, do not duplicate that chapter's hero/pricing/CTA content here. New material belongs in this chapter only when the surface is non-web (print, OOH, scripts, packaging, direct mail) or when the principle is genuinely cross-channel.

Channel character limits change over time; verify against current platform documentation before relying on the numbers in this chapter.
