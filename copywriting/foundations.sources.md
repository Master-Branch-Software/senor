# Sources — 01 Foundations

## Primary citations

- William Zinsser, _On Writing Well_ (HarperCollins, 30th anniv. ed. 2006). Source for the four principles (clarity, simplicity, brevity, humanity), the cut-50%-of-the-first-draft rule, and the read-aloud edit.
- William Strunk Jr. and E.B. White, _The Elements of Style_ (4th ed., Allyn and Bacon, 2000). Source for "Omit needless words" (Rule 17) and "Use the active voice" (Rule 14). Cornell Chronicle 50-year retrospective: <https://news.cornell.edu/stories/2009/03/omit-needless-words-elements-style-turns-50>. Public PDF of Strunk's original: <https://faculty.washington.edu/heagerty/Courses/b572/public/StrunkWhite.pdf>.
- Steven Pinker, _The Sense of Style: The Thinking Person's Guide to Writing in the 21st Century_ (Viking, 2014). Source for the curse of knowledge, classic style, conversation-with-a-reader frame.
- US Federal Plain Language Guidelines, plainlanguage.gov. <https://plainlanguage.gov/guidelines/>. Source for "write for your audience" as the first rule and the audience-first brief.
- Hemingway Editor (hemingwayapp.com). Source for the readability-grade target heuristic. Help docs: <https://hemingwayapp.com/help/docs/readability>.

## Secondary references

- Cornell University writing handouts, "Sentence variety" — burstiness as a craft principle predates LLM stylometry. <https://writing.cornell.edu/>.
- "On Writing Well" book summary, Shortform: <https://www.shortform.com/summary/on-writing-well-summary-william-zinsser>.
- "The Sense of Style" review, Josh Bernoff: <https://bernoff.com/blog/the-sense-of-style-by-steven-pinker-a-worthwhile-writing-guide>.

## Curated tool catalog

- Hemingway Editor — readability-grade and passive-voice scan. Free web app at hemingwayapp.com.
- LanguageTool — grammar and style suggestions across registers; supports custom rule sets per project.
- Vale (vale.sh) — programmable prose linter; integrates style guides as enforced rules in CI.
