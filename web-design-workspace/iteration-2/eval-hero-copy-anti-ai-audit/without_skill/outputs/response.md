# Response: hero-copy-anti-ai-audit (WITHOUT skill)

## Audit of Current Hero Copy

The current copy has several common issues:

**Original:**
> "Empower your DevOps team to streamline deployments and unlock the full potential of your pipeline. Our robust platform leverages AI to foster seamless collaboration, ensuring optimized workflows that drive innovation at scale."

### Problems identified:

1. **Vague, buzzword-heavy language** — Words like "empower," "streamline," "leverage," "seamless collaboration," and "innovation at scale" are overused to the point of meaning nothing. They don't tell the reader what the product actually does.

2. **No specific audience** — "Your DevOps team" is too broad. Senior platform engineers at Series B-D startups want to feel directly addressed.

3. **No concrete benefit or mechanism** — What does the AI actually do? What specific problem does it solve? The copy says it "ensures optimized workflows" but doesn't say how or what that looks like.

4. **"AI-powered" as a differentiator** — Saying you use AI is no longer a differentiator. Every product does. What does the AI specifically do?

5. **Passive, corporate tone** — The Sage/Hero archetype should speak more directly and with more confidence. This copy reads like it was written by committee.

## Rewritten Hero Copy

Here are a few rewrite options:

### Option 1 — Direct and outcome-focused

**Headline:**
> CI/CD observability that shows you what broke and why — before the incident ticket is filed.

**Subheadline:**
> Connect your pipeline in under 5 minutes. Senior engineers at high-growth teams use [Product] to cut mean time to resolution, not increase ticket volume.

**CTA:** Get early access / Start free

---

### Option 2 — Problem-first

**Headline:**
> Your pipeline broke. Find out why before the on-call does.

**Subheadline:**
> [Product] gives platform engineers full visibility into every deployment — what changed, what failed, and what to do next. No more war rooms at 2am.

**CTA:** See it in action

---

### Option 3 — Benefit-forward with specificity

**Headline:**
> Ship faster. Debug in minutes, not hours.

**Subheadline:**
> [Product] monitors your CI/CD pipeline end-to-end, surfaces root causes automatically, and gives your team the context to fix issues without a postmortem.

**CTA:** Connect your pipeline free

## Brand Voice Notes

For the Sage/Hero archetype targeting senior platform engineers:

- **Do use**: specific technical language they understand (pipeline, deploy, incident, MTTR, postmortem, root cause)
- **Do use**: confident, direct statements backed by specifics
- **Avoid**: "empower," "leverage," "innovate," "seamless," "holistic," "robust"
- **Tone**: Think how a very senior engineer at Stripe or Linear would talk about their own tooling — matter-of-fact, specific, no hype

The subheadline is where you earn the right to the headline. If the headline makes a claim, the subheadline delivers the mechanism or proof.
