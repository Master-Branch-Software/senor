# Response: email-microcopy-brand-voice (WITHOUT skill)

Here's a transactional email for a user who just downgraded from Pro to Free on Flint:

---

**Subject**: Your Flint plan has been updated

**From**: The Flint Team

---

Hi [First name],

We wanted to let you know that your Flint subscription has been changed from **Pro** to the **Free** plan.

We're sorry to see you step back from Pro — if there's anything we can do to help, or if you experienced any issues, please don't hesitate to reach out.

**Here's what changes on your Free plan:**

- Error tracking limited to [X events/month]
- Up to [X] team members
- [X] days of error history
- Standard support (community and documentation)

**What you'll no longer have access to:**
- Advanced error grouping by root cause
- Priority support
- Extended error history
- Custom alerting rules

**Your data is safe.** All your existing errors and history will be preserved. If you decide to upgrade back to Pro at any time, everything will be right where you left it.

**Want to come back?**

You can upgrade to Pro at any time from your account settings. We'd love to have you back when the time is right.

[Upgrade to Pro]

If you have questions about your account or ran into any issues, feel free to reply to this email or visit our [Help Center].

Thanks for using Flint,
The Flint Team

---

**Notes on this email:**

- **Subject line**: States the action taken without being alarming
- **Tone**: Empathetic and warm without being pushy or guilt-inducing
- **One CTA**: A single "Upgrade to Pro" button — keeps focus
- **Data reassurance**: Users often worry about losing their data when downgrading, so this is addressed explicitly
- **Brand voice consideration**: The "direct but not curt" voice means being clear about what changed without hedging, while "wry but not snarky" means we skip the dramatic farewell language

If you want to lean harder into the brand voice, here's an alternative opening:

> "Your Flint account is now on the Free plan. Here's what changed, what stayed, and how to get Pro back if you want it."

This is more direct and skips the "we're sorry to see you step back" framing entirely, which can feel patronizing.
